🌐 Temporary Foreign Workers — Step-by-Step Guide
1

Determine Your Stream

Agriculture uses the Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program (SAWP) for Mexico & Caribbean, or the Agricultural Stream for other countries. SAWP is most common for harvest work.

2

LMIA — Labour Market Impact Assessment

Apply to ESDC for an LMIA showing you've tried to hire Canadians first. Processing time: 10–20 business days. Current fee: $1,000 per worker position (Agriculture stream). Apply at canada.ca →

3

Recruit & Hire Workers

Work with a licensed recruitment agency or hire directly via the SAWP program through your province. HUMA-registered agents are listed at canada.ca. Never pay a recruiter fee — it's illegal.

4

Housing Requirement

You must provide or arrange suitable housing. ESDC inspectors verify housing before workers arrive. Required standards: minimum space, heat, potable water, smoke detectors. Budget $150–$300/month per worker deduction from wages.

5

Compliance Obligations

Pay at least the LMIA-approved wage. Keep payroll records 6 years. Employers can be inspected at any time. Non-compliance: fines up to $1M and bans from the program.

6

Support Resources

Contact [email protected] or call 1-855-773-0241 for TFW agriculture questions. Your provincial farm organization also provides free guidance.

⚡ SAWP Key Facts
📍 Countries: Mexico, Jamaica, Barbados, Trinidad & Tobago, and 9 other Caribbean nations
📅 Max stay: 8 months per year
💰 Employer pays: Return airfare, housing arrangement
✈️ Worker pays: Nothing — illegal to charge fees
📋 Sectors: Horticulture, livestock, grain, greenhouse
⏱ LMIA processing: 10–20 business days (priority)
📞 ESDC Helpline: 1-800-367-5693
