💰 Local Food Infrastructure Fund (LFIF)$30M Pool
The $30M LFIF supports not-for-profit and for-profit organizations building local food infrastructure. Projects must demonstrate broad community benefit.
✅ Cold storage and refrigeration infrastructure
✅ Mobile processing units (slaughter, canning, packaging)
✅ Food aggregation and distribution hubs
✅ Online market platforms for local producers
✅ Community commercial kitchens
❌ Not eligible: primary farm production equipment

🍎 CanadaGAP Certification Guide
CanadaGAP certification is required for fresh produce sold to major Canadian retailers (Loblaws, Sobeys, Metro). Annual audit cost: $800–$2,500.
Step 1: Register at canadagap.ca — free account
Step 2: Complete the Food Safety Manual template
Step 3: Book an audit with an approved auditor (~$800)
Step 4: Correct non-conformances within 30 days
Step 5: Certificate valid 1 year — annual renewal required
